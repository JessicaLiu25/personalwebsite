Architecture

Description

Beijing's architecture is a three-dimensional history book engraved with time, where every building carries the city's past and present. When the brush of an illustration glides over this land, the most touching thing is the collision and integration of old and new architectures — on one side is the Forbidden City with its red walls and yellow tiles, embodying the solemnity and profoundness accumulated over thousands of years; on the other side is the Beijing Olympic Tower full of a sense of technology, showing the innovation and edge of the contemporary era. One ancient, one modern; one soft, one rigid. They stand facing each other from afar in the long river of time, jointly outlining the unique architectural charm and urban temperament of Beijing, and also becoming the most striking visual symbol in the illustration work.
